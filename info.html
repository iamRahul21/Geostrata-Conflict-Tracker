Supporters of Iran-Backed Militias Attack U.S.-Linked Businesses in Baghdad

June 14, 2024

Supporters of Iran-backed militias launched attacks against businesses in Baghdad associated with the United States, including Kentucky Fried Chicken, Chili House, and Lee’s Famous Recipe Chicken; the attacks followed the rise of anti-American sentiment in Iraq over continued U.S. support for Israel (AP).

Turkish President Visits Iraq, Signs Deal on Iraq-Europe Transport Corridor

June 13, 2024

Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan visited Baghdad for the first time in almost thirteen years; during his visit, the two countries signed a memorandum of understanding on a transport corridor to link Iraq to Europe via railway and road, and a cooperation deal regarding the modernization of Iraqi irrigation systems (Al-Monitor).

Iraqi Prime Minister Calls for Restraint in Middle East During Washington Visit

April 15, 2024

Iraqi Prime Minister Mohammed Shia al-Sudani called for restraint in the Middle East on Monday during talks in Washington as tensions soar between Iran and Israel after Tehran's weekend strikes (Reuters). Deputy Prime Minister Muhammad Ali Tamim, who co-chaired a meeting of the U.S.-Iraq Higher Coordinating Committee with U.S. Secretary of State Antony Blinken, said Iraq was concerned about being "dragged into a wider war that will threaten international security and safety" (Middle East Monitor).
